Academic profile

Translational vision science with clinical and cognitive relevance.

Mehjabin Shirin is a Ph.D. scholar in Optometry and Vision Sciences at the University of Hyderabad. Her research focuses on visual perceptual, psychophysical, and retinal biomarkers associated with cognitive ageing, early cognitive decline, Alzheimer's disease, and dementia.

Her work brings together laboratory-based vision science, clinical vision assessment, retinal imaging, and multimodal physiological monitoring. The broader aim is to understand how changes in colour perception, gaze behaviour, pupillary dynamics, and retinal structure may contribute to non-invasive approaches for brain-health research.

Research positioning

Doctoral researcher working across colour cognition, face perception, eye tracking, OCT-based retinal imaging, neuro-ophthalmology, and dementia biomarker research.
