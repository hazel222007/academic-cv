About Me

I am a PhD Student in Optometry and Vision Sciences at the University of Hyderabad, India, investigating how vision can provide meaningful insights into cognition, ageing, and neurological health. My research brings together vision science, psychophysics, cognitive neuroscience, retinal imaging, eye tracking, and computational methods to explore the intricate relationships between perception and brain function across the lifespan. I am particularly fascinated by the potential of the eye as a window to the brain and by the opportunities it offers for advancing our understanding of human health and behaviour.

My work spans colour perception, face perception, visual attention, eye-movement behaviour, visual adaptation, and affective responses to visual stimuli. A central focus of my research is the identification of retinal and perceptual markers associated with early cognitive decline and neurodegenerative conditions such as Alzheimer's disease and related dementias. Using Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT), psychophysical assessments, eye-tracking technologies, and psychophysiological measurements, I investigate how subtle changes in visual processing may contribute to earlier detection, monitoring, and understanding of cognitive impairment. I am also interested in how colour and face perception shape emotional experience, social interaction, and everyday decision-making.

Currently, I serve as a Research Assistant with the Davos Alzheimer's Collaborative (DAC) Brain Health Initiative, contributing to international collaborative research aimed at improving approaches to brain health and dementia research. Through interdisciplinary work that bridges fundamental science and clinical application, I seek to develop practical and scientifically robust tools that can support researchers and clinicians in assessing cognitive health. My long-term goal is to advance knowledge in vision science while contributing to innovative strategies that promote healthy ageing, earlier diagnosis, and improved quality of life for individuals at risk of neurodegenerative disease.
